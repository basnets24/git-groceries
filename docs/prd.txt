OFS Food Delivery Service
 Project Requirement Document 
1.1 Background
OFS, a local organic food retailer within Downtown San Jose, is looking for a web-based solution/platform in order to expand their business model. As a newly local retailer, OFS needs a full digital platform in order to compete with established players such as Instacart and Amazon fresh while differentiating through autonomous robot delivery and cheaper pricing.

1.2 Problem Statement 
OFS cannot compete in the modern grocery market without an integrated digital platform that enables online ordering, payment processing, and inventory management along with delivery services. The lack of these services creates the following challenges. 
Revenue Loss: OFS loses potential customers who expect online shopping + delivery.
Operation Loss: OFS is currently required to manually process inventory, which is error-prone and is hard for real-time stock visibility.
Competition: Current players like Amazon Fresh and instacart have a delivery infrastructure with a significant advantage. 
This is important to bring up as Downtown San Jose’s food market is competitive, convenience is key as workers within the city have limited time for grocery shopping and prefer services that deliver to their homes or offices. Without a delivery system, OFS cannot serve customers. This directly impacts potential and market penetration. Below are the technical problems that will need to be solved. 
Weight-Aware E-commerce system
Traditional e-commerce platforms treat all products identically and calculate shipping based on flat rates or zones. OFS should implement a system where each product has its own weight attribute stored within the database, the shopping cart maintains a running total of item weights, delivery fees are calculated dynamically(rule shown later), users have real-time feedback as they edit the cart.
The shopping cart state management must track both price and weight totals with immediate updates to the frontend
Real-time inventory tracking
In store sales and online orders will deplete the same inventory, therefore there should be a system in place to track the total inventory. Without it being synchronized, customers would be able to order out of stock items, sell in-store items that are in someone's cart, and inventory diverges from the actual physical amount.
Traditional inventory systems are batch updated(once a day or week) while OFS requires transaction level accuracy to prevent overselling. 
Delivery Optimization
Autonomous vehicles are constrained by a weight limit as well as an order limit, to save time and money they must visit multiple addresses sequentially. This comes down to an optimization problem where max weight should be targeted as well as minimal customer waiting time. We can formulate the problem like so: Given N pending orders at different locations with different weights, determine which orders to batch together in order to minimize the total time wait along with using the most weight. 
Multi-Role System
The system must support three different user types and manage permissions
Customers need shopping and order tracking.
Employees need to manage inventory, this includes adding or decrementing from certain items.
Managers should get insights from the system and also be able to do what Employees should. 
Support for external dependencies
The system will use external libraries and not in isolation, for example it must integrate with payment services like credit card transactions for online payments. It must integrate with mapping services to show routes and live traffic data to optimize the routes. 
External API’s come with their own failures and inconsistencies which the system should be able to handle without exposing internal errors to users.

1.4 Functional Requirements
The following go more in depth into the actual requirements briefly stated within the problem statement. The functional requirements are as follows:
Product Catalog and Browsing
System should present the OFS product catalog 
Products should be organized into clearly defined sections such as fruits, dairy, grains, vegetables, meat, etc
Each product listing should display the product name, an image, unit price, and the weight, along with its inventory status. If the product is out of stock, a noticeable indicator should be made towards the user.
Weight Aware Shopping Cart
The cart is the main point for the customers when tracking their orders, it must go beyond price tracking. It should maintain a running sum of cumulative price and weight of all the items in the cart. 
It should update in real time, so if the user makes a change to the cart, it should be reflected. 
Delivery fee logic:
If total weight of all items in cart is below 20 pounds, delivery is free
If it's over 20 pounds, a flat 10 dollar charge will appear on the cart’s breakdown, if the customer removes items that make the total weight below 20, the charge should be removed. 
The system will enforce a max order weight of 200 pounds per order, the maximum payload of OFS’s vehicle. 	
Checkout and Payment Processing
Once a customer has finalized the cart, the system will guide them through a checkout process that collects and confirms the following:
Delivery address, full order summary, delivery fee, and total fee
A third party system should be used to handle payments
Credit card data should not be stored within any OFS database
Upon payment, the system should generate a unique order confirmation number along with an estimated delivery window allowing the customer to track their order. 
Order Tracking
Once an order is confirmed, customers should view the status of their order through the dashboard. 
It should track states like (order placed, preparing for delivery, out for delivery, and delivered)
As order progresses status should change and have an estimated arrival time 
Real Time Inventory Management
The inventory system should serve as a ground truth for all product stock levels, showing changes from online and in-store orders. When a customer completes a purchase, the system should immediately de-increment the inventory count accordingly
It should have transactional-level accuracy in order to prevent customers from buying items that are out of stock
Employees should have access to the management system where they can search and update any product by its id or name
Role-Based Access and Permissions
System should support three user roles with their own set of views and rules
Authentication should be required for all roles, requiring users to log in through a secure login page using their credentials.
The following roles are below:
Customers: access to the product catalog, shopping cart, checkout, order tracking, order history, and personal account settings
Employees: access to inventory management, search for products, update stock counts, view low-stock items, pending orders, fulfillment status
Managers: everything employees and customers have, they should also have large overviews and total gain or loss through the store, the data should be enough to generate reports based on the store. 

1.5 User Stories
As a customer, I want to browse products and see their price, weight, and availability, so that I can select items to purchase.
As a customer, I want to manage my shopping cart with real-time price, weight totals, and delivery fee calculation, so that I know the exact cost before checkout.
As a customer, I want to complete checkout with address confirmation and secure payment processing, so that my order can be placed successfully.
As for the system, I want to validate and update inventory when an order is placed, so that customers cannot purchase out-of-stock items and stock levels remain accurate.
As a customer, I want to track my order status and delivery estimate after confirmation, so that I know when my order will arrive.

1.6 Product Workflow 
The customer workflow begins with browsing products and adding items to the cart. The cart updates totals in real time, including weight-based delivery fees. After checkout and payment confirmation, the order is stored and inventory is updated.
The operational workflow then schedules the order for delivery. The system groups orders into trips while respecting vehicle constraints and computes optimized routes using mapping services. Delivery progress updates the order status so customers and staff can monitor fulfillment.

1.7 Non-Functional Requirements
The platform must maintain responsive performance so customers can browse and checkout without delays. Security must be enforced through encrypted communication, password hashing, and role-based authorization. The system must ensure data integrity, particularly for inventory and transactions. External API failures must be handled gracefully so that users are not exposed to internal errors. 

1.8 Success Criteria 
The system will be successful if customers can complete online purchases end to end, staff can manage inventory reliably, delivery trips respect robot constraints, and routes are optimized effectively. Achieving these outcomes will allow OFS to compete in the local market while maintaining operational efficiency.
